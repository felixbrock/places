# Guide

Description: A guide to non-obvious London highlights — meant to complement the must-visit landmarks (Tower Bridge, Westminster + Big Ben, Shakespeare's Globe, Tate Modern, Millennium Bridge, the big parks…) rather than repeat them. The idea is to focus on one or two areas max per day and pick what appeals from there. Two lenses on the same set of places: this file groups them by **type** (cafes, restaurants, markets, streets, …) so you can browse by what you're in the mood for; the linked Google Maps list groups them by **area** so you can plan a day around one neighbourhood.

## Nature

- Regent's Canal
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Regent%27s+Canal+London
  - What it is: 14 km canal you can walk from Little Venice through Camden and Hackney down to Limehouse Basin.
  - Tags: nature, walk, canal
- Blitz Memorial (Wapping)
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Blitz+Memorial+Wapping+High+Street+London
  - What it is: Small WWII civilian-bombing memorial sculpture along Wapping High Street.
  - Tags: nature, memorial, wapping, history
- Hampstead Heath (and Hampstead Village)
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hampstead+Heath+London
  - What it is: 320-hectare wild park in north London — bathing ponds, Parliament Hill view, Kenwood House — paired with the Georgian streets of Hampstead Village just south.
  - Tags: nature, park, walk, view, hampstead
- Highgate Cemetery
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Highgate+Cemetery+Swain%27s+Lane+London
  - What it is: Victorian cemetery on Swain's Lane in north London — overgrown, gothic, with Karl Marx and George Eliot among the graves.
  - Tags: nature, cemetery, history, walk, highgate
- Mudchute Park and Farm
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mudchute+Park+and+Farm+London
  - What it is: 13-hectare city farm on the Isle of Dogs with sheep, pigs, horses and views straight up at the Canary Wharf towers.
  - Tags: nature, farm, park, isle-of-dogs, free
- Horniman Museum and Gardens
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Horniman+Museum+and+Gardens+Forest+Hill+London
  - What it is: Free Forest Hill museum (anthropology, natural history, musical instruments — and the famous overstuffed walrus) set in 16 acres of gardens with panoramic views over central London.
  - Tags: nature, museum, gardens, view, forest-hill, free

## Buildings/Architecture

- St Pancras Station
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=St+Pancras+Station+London
  - What it is: Victorian Gothic terminus on Euston Road; Eurostar departures and the Renaissance Hotel staircase.
  - Tags: architecture, station, gothic, kings-cross
- Sky Garden
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sky+Garden+20+Fenchurch+Street+London
  - What it is: Free public roof garden atop 20 Fenchurch Street ("Walkie-Talkie") — book a slot in advance.
  - Tags: architecture, view, garden, city, free
- Barbican Centre
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Barbican+Centre+Silk+Street+London
  - What it is: Brutalist arts complex with concert halls, cinemas, galleries and the conservatory.
  - Tags: architecture, brutalist, arts, city
- Bank (City of London)
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bank+Underground+Station+London
  - What it is: Bank junction at the heart of the City — Bank of England, Royal Exchange and Mansion House around the station entrance.
  - Tags: architecture, city, history, station
- Westbourne Grove Church
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Westbourne+Grove+Church+Ledbury+Road+London
  - What it is: Striking white-stuccoed Victorian church with shops inside at the corner of Westbourne Grove and Ledbury Road in Notting Hill.
  - Tags: architecture, church, notting-hill
- Trellick Tower (Careful around it; some corners can be dangerous)
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trellick+Tower+Golborne+Road+London
  - What it is: Ernő Goldfinger's 31-storey 1972 Brutalist tower above Golborne Road — Grade II\*-listed and instantly recognisable.
  - Tags: architecture, brutalist, golborne, notting-hill
- Greenwich Foot Tunnel North
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Greenwich+Foot+Tunnel+North+Entrance+London
  - What it is: Domed Edwardian rotunda at Island Gardens marking the north entrance to the 1902 pedestrian tunnel under the Thames to Greenwich.
  - Tags: architecture, history, isle-of-dogs, walk, river-thames
- Lloyd's Building
  - https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Lloyd%27s+Building+1+Lime+Street+London
  - What it is: Richard Rogers' 1986 "inside-out" high-tech tower on Lime Street — lifts, ducts and pipes all running up the outside; Grade I-listed.
  - Tags: architecture, high-tech, city, history
